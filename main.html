Game Rules

Overview

Your objective is to build four metro lines across Budapest—one per round. Each line begins from its designated starting station, marked with a grey outline. You extend the line by drawing cards and connecting valid stations while following the placement rules. After all four lines are completed, your network is scored.

Drawing Cards

Each round includes up to eight card draws. A card determines the type of station you can connect to (A, B, C, D, or ?). “?” cards allow any station type. Drawing the switch card lets you continue the line from either of your line’s endpoints. Eligible stations are highlighted automatically. If a card is drawn and no valid move exists, the round ends.

Placing Segments

Segments must be straight: horizontal, vertical, or diagonal. A segment cannot pass through another station, cross existing segments, or connect to a station already used by the current line. When you place a valid segment, the newly connected station becomes your new endpoint.

Round Progression

A round concludes when eight cards have been drawn or when a card leaves you without any valid connections. When a round ends, the next metro line begins at its own starting station. After four rounds, the game ends.

Scoring

When all four lines are completed, the game calculates your final score. Each line earns points based on district coverage and station distribution. Additional points are awarded for crossing the Danube, touching railway stations, and forming junctions between lines. Your final score and time are displayed and stored in the scoreboard.
